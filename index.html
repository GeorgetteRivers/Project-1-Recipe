Chicken Salad on Butter Lettuce

Have you ever had a craving for chicken salad?


Chicken salad is universal and a great way for you to utilize leftover chicken. The key secret to a basic or savory chicken salad are the ingredients. Many recipes require a mayonase base but, there are alternatives like yogurt, sour cream and even one that uses a vinegarette base. Just as important, is the foundation as this too can also make or break your craving. Chicken salad could be eaten alone or made with a few slices of bread; even toasted or maybe a crescent. Possibly, on a bed of butter lettuce, or even a cracker. However, you choose to eat chicken salad may it be alone or on a foundation of your choosing. What-ever the preference you like it will be divine.

This is a family recipe one to enjoy! The recipe can be made with a poached, boiled or roasted chicken. In fact, you could even purchase a supermarket roasted chicken. If case you need to save time; the supermarket roasters are a quick short-cut. I must admit that I prefer roasting the chicken as a best cooking method because, it allows the chicken to stay tender and moist. Also, remember you can shred or dice the chicken; what ever your preference you choose; it will change the texture and taste of the chicken salad.

Grocery List If Roasting Chicken

Roaster Ingredients

  • 5-6 pound chicken roaster
  • 4 whole carrots
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 large lemon
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2-4 tablspoons of olive oil
  • 4 sprigs of Parsley
  • 8 sprigs of Tyme
  • 4 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of freshly cracker-pepper


Cooking Directions for Roasting the Chicken


If you choose to roast the chicken a 5-6 pound roaster is adequate. Preheat oven to 425 degreees. Remove giblets and discard. Pat dry your roaster (tie the legs with cooking twine in front of the bird and place the wings underneath the body of the roaster; This ensures even cooking). Generoulsy salt and pepper the roaster (as per diet allows). Melt 2 tablespoons of butter. Pour melted butter over the roaster. Season again.

I do use vegetables for a foundation to place under the roaster as it cooks. About 4 carrots cut into 4" pieces, One large onion cut into chunks similar in size to the carrots. One lemon cut into quarters. About 8 sprigs on fresh tyme. Four springs of fresh parsley. One whole garlic cut in half. (I drizzle olive oil onto the head of garlic and season with salt and peper or seasonings of your choice). Place the carrots, onions and lemon in a bowl and drizzle about two tablespoons of olive oil onto the vegtables, add some salt and pepper and mix them well.

Place the vegetables onto a pan adequate in size to accomadate the roaster (cast iron works well) than place the fresh herbs on top of the vegtables. Place the roaster on top of the vegetables and cook uncovered until chicken temperature has reached 165 degrees (place thermometer inside leg preferably to get accurate reading) or until the chicken juices run clear. Please note that if the top of the roaster is cooking quicker than the body place aluminum foil over that area and continue cooking. Likewise if the top of roaster is not brown enough than move the roaster pan a little closer to the top of oven. Depending upon how you like your skin cooked.
